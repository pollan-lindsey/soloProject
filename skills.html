5 New Skills Added to the 2022-24 Code of Points

Acronyms:

BB = balance beam

FX = floor exercise

UB = uneven bars

WAG = women's artistic gymnastics

FIG = International Gymnastics Federation

CoP = code of points

The Yeo (Vault)

Difficulty: 5.8

First competed successfully by Korean gymnast Yeo Seojeong at the 2019 Korea Cup, the Yeo is a Handspring fwd on – stretched salto fwd with 2/1 twist (720°) off. It is one of the most difficult vaults in the world.

Bonus Fact: In men’s artistic gymnastics, this skill is named after her father, Yeo Hong-chul.

The Martins (Unveven Bars)

Difficulty: F

First competed successfully by Portuguese gymnast Ana Filipa Martins at the 2021 European Championships, the Martins is a clear hip circle on HB with counter straddle over the HB with ½ turn (180°) to hang in mixed L-grip.

Common Name: Hindorff ½

Bonus Fact: Martins is the first Portuguese gymnast to get a skill named after her.

The Derwael-Fenton (Uneven Bars)

Difficulty: F

First competed successfully by British gymnast Georgia-Mae Fenton and Belgian gymnast Nina Derwael at the 2017 Artistic Gymnastics World Championships, the Derwael-Fenton is a stalder bwd on HB with counter straddle reverse hecht over HB with ½ (180°) turn to hang in mixed L-grip. The video shows Georgia-Mae Fenton competing the skill

Bonus Fact: The Derwael-Fenton is the first skill in the WAG CoP to be named after multiple gymnasts.

The Sugihara

Difficulty: E

First competed successfully by Japanese gymnast Sugihara Aiko at the 2017 Artistic Gymnastics World Championships, the Sugihara is 2/1 turn (720°) with free leg held upward in 180° split position throughout turn.

Common names: Memmel on beam; double Y-turn

Bonus Fact: Because it is a dance skill, the highest difficulty the Sugihara can have is an E. However many gymnastics fans feel that it is too difficult to be considered an E-difficulty skill. The same skill on the floor (Memmel) has a D-difficulty.

The Maldonado

Difficulty: F

First competed successfully by Puerto Rican gymnast Andrea Maldonado at the 2019 Cottbus World Cup, the Maldonado is a salto fwd stretched with 3/1 twist.

Common name: front triple full/front triple twist

Bonus Fact: This is one of my favorite skills and I will never stop talking about how undervalued it is.
